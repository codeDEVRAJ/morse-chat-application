What is Morse Code?

Morse code is a method used in telecommunication to encode text characters as standardized sequences of two different signal durations, called dots and dashes. It was developed by Samuel Morse and Alfred Vail in the 1830s and became widely used for telegraph communication.

Basic Rules of Morse Code

  • A dot (.) represents a short signal or "dit".
  • A dash (-) represents a long signal or "dah".
  • The space between parts of the same letter is 1 unit.
  • The space between letters is 3 units.
  • The space between words is 7 units.
